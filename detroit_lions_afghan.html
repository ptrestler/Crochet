At a Glance

  • Worked flat, sideways: 167 rows wide × 140 HDC tall
  • 4 colors: Feather Gray (background), Black, White, Sapphire blue
  • Rows 1–17 & 151–167 are solid Feather Gray; the design lives in rows 18–150
  • Every row totals 140 stitches

Materials

  • Caron Simply Soft — Feather Gray × 5
  • Caron Simply Soft — Black × 1
  • Caron Simply Soft — White × 1
  • Big Twist Value — Sapphire × 3
  • 7 mm crochet hook (main)
  • 5 mm crochet hook
  • Scissors, measuring tape, stitch markers
  • Optional backing: Minky fleece, ~52" × 72"
  • Optional: sharp tool to punch holes in backing fabric
Any worsted-weight yarn works; the designer doesn't recommend going thicker than a size 2 sport weight.

Pattern Notes

Getting Started

Begin with a starting chain of 142. HDC in the 2nd chain from the hook and across for a total of 140 HDC. This is the first row of your blanket and becomes the left edge.

At the end of each row, ch 1 and turn. This chain does not count as a stitch — it just adds height. Every row totals 140 stitches.

Even rows are the Right Side (front); odd rows are the Wrong Side (back). The row tracker below shows this for each row.
Changing Colors
1

Work the last stitch of the old color until you have two loops left on your hook.

2

Yarn over with the new color and pull through both loops to complete the stitch — this transitions the color seamlessly.

3

Continue with the new color: start your next HDC as usual (insert hook, yarn over, pull up a loop — 3 loops on hook — yarn over and pull through all three).

4

Carry yarn and make all color changes on the wrong side to keep the front neat.

Finishing

SC Border

At the end of row 167: DO NOT fasten off, DO NOT ch 1, DO NOT turn. You'll work a single crochet border around the whole blanket.

1

Place 1 SC in the chain stitch at the end of each row down the side. If you plan to add backing, count your stitches on each side exactly.

2

At the corner, place 2–3 SC in the corner stitch.

3

Along the starting-chain edge, place 1 SC in the back loop of each stitch. At the next corner, 2–3 SC.

4

Down the final side, 1 SC in each row-end chain. 2–3 SC in the last corner, then SL to the first HDC of row 167.

No backing? Keep working rounds of SC until the border reaches your desired thickness, then tie off and weave in all ends. You're done!
Optional

Adding a Fleece Backing (Preferred)

1

Tie off and trim ends — no need to weave them in, the backing covers them. Lay the blanket on the backing fabric and cut the fabric about 1 inch larger on all sides.

2

Punch holes around the fabric edge — the same number per side as you have border stitches on the blanket.

3

Insert hook through a hole from WS to RS and pull up a loop of matching yarn. Fold the fabric edge over (both right sides facing out), yarn over and pull up a loop to attach.

4

SC along the folded edge using the holes as base stitches, 2–3 SC in each corner. Keep the same stitch count as the blanket edge.

5

Match the wrong sides of blanket and backing, align the SC edges, and SL the two pieces together all the way around. Tie off well and weave in the final ends.

Care

1

Hand-wash in cold water with mild detergent. Gently swish — don't wring or scrub.

2

Squeeze gently, then roll in a dry towel, pressing to absorb water.

3

Lay flat to dry, reshape, and allow ~24 hours. Flip and repeat if still damp.
