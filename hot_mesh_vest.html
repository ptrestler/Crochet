Hot Mesh Vest

Made-to-Measure Crochet Vest Pattern

Materials

  • Hook: 2.5–3.00 mm (main)
  • Hook: 1.5 mm (for ribbing)
  • Aunt Lydia's Classic 10 thread (lace weight / WPI ~30)
  • Yardage: Size S ≈ 450 yds (~1 ball)
  • Scissors
  • Measuring tape
  • Stitch markers
You can substitute any yarn up to size 2 Sport weight. The pattern is made-to-measure so it works with any yarn — just swatch first and adjust as needed.

Gauge

Hook4" × 4" swatch
2.5–3.00 mm12 rows × 6.5 holes
Because this is a made-to-measure pattern, gauge affects the drape and feel but not the finished size — your measurements drive the math. Still worth swatching to check that you like the fabric density.

Measurements You'll Need

Take these measurements before you start. Write them down — you'll refer back to them throughout.

MeasurementWhere to measure
BustAround the fullest part of your chest
WaistAround the narrowest part of your torso
Length 1Bottom hem up to where armholes begin
Length 2Armhole level up to neckline
Length 3Neckline up to the top of the shoulder
Width (W)Shoulder to neck (width of one shoulder strap)

Abbreviations (US Terms)

  • Sc Single crochet
  • Ch Chain
  • Sl st Slip stitch
  • Dc Double crochet
  • Sc blo Single crochet in back loop only
  • St Stitch
  • Sk Skip one stitch
Part 1

Body — Worked in the Round

Starting Chain
1

Calculate your starting chain length N:

Formula
N = (Bust + Waist) ÷ 2
Round N to the nearest multiple of 4, then add 1 (for the turning chain).

Make a chain that measures N inches / cm in length. The number of chains must be a multiple of 4, plus ch 1.

If you want a looser fit, add a few extra chains to N before rounding.
2

Slip stitch into the first chain to form a ring. Be careful not to twist the chain.

Lay the chain flat on a table and check it isn't twisted before closing the ring.
Round 1
3

Ch 3 (counts as 1st dc). *Ch 3, sk 3, dc 1 in the next ch.* Repeat * to * to the end of the round. After the last complete repeat: ch 3, sk 3, sl st into the 3rd ch of the 1st dc. Turn.

Round 2
4

Ch 3 (counts as 1st dc), ch 2, sc 1 in the chain space, ch 2, dc 1 in the dc st. *Ch 2, sc 1 in ch space, ch 2, dc 1 in dc st.* Repeat * to * to end. After the last complete repeat: ch 2, sc 1 in ch space, ch 2, sl st into the 3rd ch of the 1st dc. Turn.

Round 3
5

Ch 3 (counts as 1st dc). *Ch 3, sk ch2, sk 1 sc, sk ch2, dc 1 in dc st.* Repeat * to * to end. After the last ch 3, sl st into the 3rd ch of the 1st dc. Turn.

Continue to Length 1
6

Repeat Rounds 2 and 3 alternately until the piece measures Length 1 (bottom hem to armhole start). Remember to account for approximately 1 inch / 2 cm of ribbing that will be added at the bottom.

7

Your final round must be a Round 3 (the ch 3 / hole round). Do not cut your yarn.

Part 2

Dividing Front & Back Panels

8

Count the total number of large holes (the pattern repeats) across your last round. Call this N.

9

Calculate your panel sizes:

Formula
X = N − 2   (skip 1 hole on each side for armholes)
A = X ÷ 2
Front panel = A + 1 holes
Back panel = A − 1 holes

Example: 48 holes total → X = 46, A = 23 → Front = 24 holes, Back = 22 holes

If you prefer a more narrow upper body, skip more than 1 hole on each side. Skipping 1 gives a boxier, wider shoulder line.
10

Count A + 1 holes from your hook (these are the front panel). Place a stitch marker on the next hole.

11

Count A − 1 holes from the hole next to the stitch marker (these are the back panel). Place a stitch marker on the next hole.

The 2 remaining holes (one on each side) are the skipped armhole gaps.

Part 3

Front Panel & Shoulders

Front Panel Body (to neckline)

Switch from working in the round to working in rows, across the front panel only.

12

Row 1: Ch 3 (counts as 1st dc). *Ch 2, sc 1 in chain space, ch 2, dc 1 in dc st.* Repeat * to * to the end of the front panel. Turn.

13

Row 2: Ch 3 (counts as 1st dc). *Ch 3, sk ch2, sk 1 sc, sk ch2, dc 1 in dc st.* Repeat * to * to end. Turn.

14

Repeat Rows 1–2 until the piece measures Length 2 (from armhole level up to the neckline).

Front Shoulder (first side)
15

Work only across the shoulder width W (from one shoulder to neck). Continue repeating Rows 1–2 for Length 3 (neckline to top of shoulder).

16

Ch 1, pull tight, and cut yarn leaving a short tail.

Front Shoulder (second side)
17

Attach yarn on the opposite shoulder. Work identically to Steps 15–16. Ch 1, pull tight, cut yarn.

Part 4

Back Panel & Shoulders

18

Attach yarn at the back panel. Work exactly as the front panel (Steps 12–14), but work 2 extra rows on the back panel before starting the shoulder.

19

For each back shoulder: work 2 fewer rows compared to the front shoulder. This compensates for the extra body rows so both panels meet evenly at the top.

20

Work both back shoulders the same way as the front (Steps 15–17). Ch 1, pull tight, cut yarn on each side.

Joining the Shoulders
21

Turn the vest inside out. Line up the front and back shoulder pieces. Slip stitch the two layers together, inserting the hook through both loops of one stitch from each layer at a time. Repeat for the second shoulder.

Part 5

Ribbing

Add ribbing around the bottom hem, both armholes, and the neckline.

22

Attach yarn into any space or chain at the edge you're adding ribbing to. Work with the outside of the garment facing you.

23

Ch 7 (or more chains for a wider ribbing band). Switch to your 1.5 mm hook.

24

Row 1: Sc blo in the 2nd ch from hook and in each ch across — 6 sc. Sl st 2 into 2 of the spaces/chains on the vest edge, then flip.

Around the armholes there are no defined stitches — insert the hook into the dc posts instead, working 2 sl sts per dc row.
25

Row 2: Sk the 2 sl sts, sc blo in each sc across — 6 sc. Ch 1, flip back.

26

Repeat Rows 1–2 around the entire edge, working in back loops only throughout. When you return to the start, connect the ribbing ends by slip stitching from the inside.

27

Weave in all ends. Repeat ribbing on all remaining edges (armholes and neckline).

Caring Tips

1

Hand-wash in cold water with a mild detergent. Gently swish the vest through the water — do not wring or scrub.

2

After rinsing, gently squeeze (do not wring) and roll the vest in a dry towel. Press firmly as you roll to absorb excess water.

3

Lay flat to dry on a clean dry towel or rack. Re-shape the vest as needed. Allow 24 hours. If still damp, flip onto a fresh towel, re-shape, and allow another 24 hours.
