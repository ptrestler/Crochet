Adjustment Summary

  • Original hook: 3.5mm  →  New hook: 2.5mm
  • Original yarn: Sport weight  →  New yarn: Big Twist Baby Bunny (Weight 1, Super Fine)
  • Original gauge: 2×2 in = 9 rows × 4 wattle sts  →  New gauge: 12 rows × 5 wattle sts
  • All stitch counts scaled ×5/4 · All row counts scaled ×4/3 · Size Medium only
✦ Left-handed edition — all left/right directional references have been mirrored throughout.

Materials

  • Hook: 2.5mm
  • Big Twist Baby Bunny yarn (Weight 1, Super Fine) — 200–250g for size M
  • Tape measure
  • Stitch markers (at least 4)
  • Scissors
  • Row counter (strongly recommended — wattle stitch is difficult to count)

To make a gauge swatch: Chain 33, then follow the Wattle Stitch instructions until you have at least 15 rows. Measure a 2×2 inch section — it should contain 12 rows and 5 wattle stitches. Adjust hook size if needed before starting the pattern.

How to Do the Wattle Stitch

The wattle stitch is composed of a cluster of sc, ch, and dc separated by 2 sts, with sc on both ends of the row.

1 cluster of sc–ch1–dc = 1 ws (wattle stitch)

Row 1
1

Create a slip knot. Chain in multiples of 3.

2

Sc on the 2nd ch from the hook. On the next ch, make sc–ch1–dc. These 3 stitches (sc–ch1–dc) are all placed in the same ch.

3

*Skip 2 ch, then make sc–ch1–dc on the next.* Repeat ** until you reach the last 3 chains of the row.

4

When you reach the last 3 chains: skip 2, then sc on the last ch.

Row 2 (and all subsequent rows)
5

Ch1. Flip your work. Sc on top of the sc from the previous row.

6

Make sc–ch1–dc on every ch1 space of the row. Sc on top of the last sc from the previous row.

From here, Row 2 (Steps 5–6) is repeated throughout the pattern. It will be referred to as the non-increase/non-decrease row.
Part 1

Front Panel

The stitch counts shown are for Size M only.
Straps & Neckline (Rows 1–11)
1

Create a slip knot. Ch 30. (This corresponds to the width of the strap.)

2

Sc on the 2nd ch from the hook. *On the next ch, make a ws, then skip 2 ch.* Repeat ** until you reach the last 3 chains. Skip 2, sc on the last ch. This counts as Row 1.

→ Total ws: 9
3

Ch1. Flip your work. Sc on top of the sc from the previous row. Make a ws on every ch1 space. Sc on top of the last sc.

4

Repeat Step 3 until you have a total of 11 rows. This is your strap length. The wattle stitch stretches lengthwise when worn — consider this when measuring. You may adjust in multiples of 2 rows based on your preferences.

5

After the last sc on your last row, ch 43. Use a stitch marker to hold the last chain and cut yarn, leaving a 1–2 inch tail. This chain becomes your neckline. You may adjust in multiples of 3 + 1 (e.g. 43, 46, 49…) to ensure the last chain lands a wattle stitch.

6

Repeat Steps 1–4 for the second strap. Do not cut the yarn — this will be your working yarn.

7

Both straps should face you the same way when connecting. Take the chain from Strap 1 (make sure it is not twisted) and slip stitch onto the 1st sc of the last row of Strap 2. Fasten off the tail, then return to the working yarn from Step 6.

Connecting Row (Row 12)
8

Sc on top of the sc from the previous row. Make a ws on every ch1 space until you reach the last ch1 space of the strap.

9

Begin working across the connecting chain. Skip the sc after the last ch1 space, then make a ws on the 1st ch after the sc. *Skip 2 ch, make a ws on the next.* Repeat ** until you reach the end of the connecting chain.

10

When you reach the other strap: skip sc, make a ws on every ch1 space of the row. Sc on top of the last sc.

→ Total ws: 33
Increases (Rows 13–49)
11

Ch1. Flip your work. Sc on top of the sc from the previous row. Make a ws on every ch1 space. Sc on top of the last sc. (Non-increase row.) Repeat for 7 more rows.

→ Total rows: 19
12

Continue with 1 ws per ch1 space until the other marker. Make 2 ws on the same ch1 space (increase). Continue to the end. Sc on top of the last sc.

→ Total ws: 35

13

Tie (Final Rows)
16

Repeat Step 3 (non-increase row) until you reach the last 10 rows of your back panel. Your last completed non-increase row must be an odd number before starting the tie.

Count your front panel rows and subtract 10 to find when to begin Step 17. e.g. front panel = 73 rows → 73 − 10 = 63, so repeat Step 3 until you have 63 total rows (odd ✓), then start Step 17 on row 64.
17

Begin the tie. Since your previous row is odd, your hook should be on the side opposite the armhole. Ch 75. If you want longer ties to form a ribbon bow, ch more in multiples of 3.

18

Sc on the 2nd ch from the hook. *On the next ch, make a ws, then skip 2 ch.* Repeat ** until you reach the sc from the previous row. Skip sc. Make a ws on every ch1 space of the row. Sc on top of the last sc.

Ch1, flip your work. Sc on top of the sc. Make a ws on every ch1 space of the row. Sc on top of the last sc.

20

Repeat Steps 1–20 to make the second back panel.

Finishing

Connect all panels together (sides and straps) using single crochet. For the sides, connect starting from the bottom edge up to — but not including — the last increase row where you placed the stitch markers.

Optional: Add an sc edging around the entire top for cleaner, more refined edges.

Congratulations — your Ivy Top is complete!

Additional Tips

If your back panels droop when worn, try one or more of the following:

  • Shorten the overall length of the top. Keep it a few inches above the navel.
  • Make thicker back ties by adding more rows during the tie section — repeat Step 19 additional times in Step 20 (add rows in multiples of 2).
  • Add an sc edging around the back, and sc2tog at any drooping spots to gently cinch them in.
Always try the top on as you go — the wattle stitch stretches when worn, so in-progress fitting gives the best results.
Since this adjusted pattern uses a much finer yarn, your finished fabric will be denser and drapier than the original. This is expected.
